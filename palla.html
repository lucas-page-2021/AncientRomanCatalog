Outdoor mantle for Roman women

The palla was a rectangular wrap or mantle worn over a tunic or stola when a woman went out in public. It could be draped over the shoulders, wrapped around the body, or pulled over the head.

It functioned as both practical outerwear and a visible marker of modesty and status. Finer examples could be dyed, bordered, or woven in more luxurious fabrics.

Estimated price: 500 to 7,500 denarii (2,000 to 30,000 sestertii)

Estimate based on late-Roman clothing scholarship cited in Harlow via Women in Antiquity; plain wraps sat at the low end, while dyed or patterned pallae were much costlier.

Historical Notes

Roman prices changed over time and by quality, so this figure is an estimate rather than a single empire-wide price tag.

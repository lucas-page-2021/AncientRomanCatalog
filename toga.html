Formal wool garment of the Roman citizen

The toga was a large draped outer garment worn for civic, ceremonial, and public life. It was heavy, highly structured, and strongly associated with official Roman identity.

Different versions signaled rank, including plain white forms, magistrate togas with purple, and highly ceremonial variants.

Estimated price: 1,000 to 15,000 denarii (4,000 to 60,000 sestertii)

Inference from late Roman price ceilings for wool outerwear and dyed garments in Diocletian’s edict; a plain toga would be far below a purple-striped luxury example.

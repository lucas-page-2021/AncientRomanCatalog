Shoes

Leather calcei and tied Roman footwear

Closed leather shoes, often called calcei, were worn outdoors by citizens and paired with formal dress. They covered the foot and ankle and were secured with crossed leather laces.

Roman footwear varied by class, purpose, and gender, from simple sandals to more formal closed shoes.

Estimated price: 60 to 120 denarii (240 to 480 sestertii)

Based directly on Diocletian’s Price Edict entries for men’s, women’s, and higher-grade sandals or shoes in 301 CE.
